Ethics of Care

The ethics of care emphasizes the importance of human connections, empathy, interdependence, and mutual responsibility. In an increasingly individualistic world, it is urgent to recover the value of care as the foundation of a more just, equitable, and sustainable society.

Caregiving tasks not only encompass physical or emotional support but also involve listening, patience, mindfulness, and commitment. These tasks have historically been associated with women, which has led to significant inequality in their distribution and recognition.

Why is it important to revalue care?

  • Care is essential to the life and well-being of all people.
  • Recognizing this strengthens a more humane and supportive society.
  • Promote a culture of respect, listening and collaboration.
  • Challenge traditional gender roles assigned to care.
  • Generates new forms of leadership based on empathy.

Care and community

Caregiving is not only an individual responsibility, but also a collective task. In community contexts, mutual care can be a tool for social transformation and resistance to inequalities.

Educating from an ethic of care allows us to value shared time, affection, and the invisible work that sustains everyday life. This vision fosters social commitment and respect for diversity in all its forms.

The ethics of care in education is key to building equal relationships, promoting respect for diversity, and valuing affect as an essential part of human development.
