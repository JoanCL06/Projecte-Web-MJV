Gender Relations

Understanding the impact of stereotypes on society and education

Gender Relations

Gender relations are social constructs that determine the ways men and women interact within a society. These relations are not natural, but rather learned from childhood through family, school, the media, and other social environments.

Stereotypes and gender roles

Gender stereotypes assign specific characteristics, behaviors, and roles based on sex. For example, men are expected to be strong and rational, while women are expected to be sensitive and nurturing. These stereotypes limit people's opportunities for personal and professional development.

Impact on education

In the educational setting, gender relations influence how boys and girls are treated, what subjects they are encouraged to study, and how their aspirations are shaped. Coeducational education must challenge these patterns and promote equity in the classroom.

What do we propose?

  • Review curricular content from a gender perspective.
  • Train teachers in equality and coeducation.
  • Make female role models visible in all areas.
  • Promote respect, empathy, and co-responsibility.
